If people would be asked to describe me, you’d get different answers depending on who you ask. Some would call me successful. Others would go to my technical background, praising my qualifications or experience. I’ve always thought that who you are isn’t where you come from, or how many degrees you have. It’s not what you do for work, how much you earn, where you live or what you believe in. I’m a normal person with big dreams.

When I started building IndiaNIC, I focused on ‘What can I offer?’, and ‘How may I serve?’. Looking back, I’m smiling. Smiling because I feel like I’ve lived in purpose. My goal was to help people who needed me and constantly learn, so that I can continue doing it as much as possible.
